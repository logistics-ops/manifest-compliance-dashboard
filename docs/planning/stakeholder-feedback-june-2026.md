# Stakeholder Feedback - June 2026

## Current Product Direction

Manifest Operations Center should stay compliance-focused in the near term. The dashboard should emphasize audit readiness, carrier documents, DQ files, vehicle maintenance, compliance alerts, notifications, and task follow-up.

Operational modules should not dominate the primary navigation until stakeholders are ready to reintroduce them.

## Navigation Decisions

- Loads should remain a future module and appear only as `Loads (Coming Soon)` when shown.
- Loads should not be clickable from primary navigation.
- Invoices should stay hidden from navigation.
- Activity should stay hidden from navigation.
- Weather should stay hidden from navigation.
- Archives should stay hidden from navigation.
- Future navigation should include Inspection Reports.
- Future navigation should include Safety Scores.

## Terminology Decisions

- Rename `Critical Blockers` to `Critical Compliance Issues`.
- Rename `Vehicle Readiness` to `Vehicle Maintenance`.
- Avoid dashboard wording that makes the product feel load-management, billing, archive, or dispatch-first.

## High-Priority Future Feature

No-login carrier upload links are a high-priority future feature. The goal is to let carriers submit required compliance documents through a secure link without needing to log in.

Future planning should define:

- Link expiration rules.
- Carrier/document scoping.
- Upload path validation.
- Audit logging.
- Abuse protection.
- Admin review workflow after upload.

## Future Modules

Future modules requested by stakeholders:

- Safety Score Tracking
- Safety Coaching
- Driver Logs/HOS
- Pre-Trips/Post-Trips

These should be planned as future phases and should not distract from the current compliance-focused dashboard.
